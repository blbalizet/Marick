What Good Looks Like

What Good Sentences Look Like

Here's a strong example. Someone wrote about playing Fortnite:

✓ This works
Playing Fortnite is exciting because every game feels like a new adventure. You jump from the Battle Bus, land on the island, and quickly look for weapons and shields while watching out for other players. As the safe circle gets smaller, the game becomes more intense, and you have to move, build, and think fast to stay alive. It feels great when you win a fight or help your teammates, and getting a Victory Royale is really rewarding. Even when you lose, you learn a little more each time, so the game stays fun and makes you want to try again.

Notice what's happening:

✓Each sentence does one job.
✓They build on each other — there's a flow.
✓There's a beginning, a middle, and a point.
What to Avoid

What We'd Want to Avoid

Here's the same idea — written in a way that won't earn full credit:

✗ This doesn't work
Fortnite is fun. I like it. It's exciting. You land and get guns. The circle moves. I win sometimes. It's a good game.

Seven sentences — but they don't go anywhere.

Each one is a dead end.

Your teacher isn't counting sentences. They're reading for thought.

Give them something to read.
