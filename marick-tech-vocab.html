Packet

Tech Term 1 of 9
A packet is a small unit of data transmitted over a network. When information is sent across the internet, it doesn't travel in one big chunk — it's broken into packets that travel independently and reassemble at the destination. Routers are responsible for directing these packets to the right place.

Tech Term 2 of 9
Router
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 2 of 9
A router passes information between one or more computer networks. It acts as a gateway — sitting between the internet connection coming in through a modem and the local network your devices use to communicate. When a packet arrives, the router identifies where it's going and calculates the best path to get it there.

Game Dev Term 1 of 4
Algorithm

Game Dev Term 1 of 4
An algorithm is a step-by-step set of rules a computer follows to solve a problem or complete a task. In games, algorithms power NPC movement, damage calculations, and physics. A routing table — the set of rules a router uses to direct data — is itself an algorithm.

Standalone

Tech Term 3 of 9
Routers can be standalone — a physical box you can hold — or virtual, meaning the router is a software instance running on a machine rather than existing as separate hardware. Both do the same job. One is physical, one isn't.

Tech Term 4 of 9
Firmware
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 4 of 9
Firmware is a type of software embedded into hardware devices during manufacturing to control their basic functions. It runs before the operating system, is stored in non-volatile memory (so it survives power loss), and acts as a bridge between hardware and software. You can't see it working — but it's what makes a device know how to be a device.
Fill in the Blank
___________ is a type of software embedded into hardware during manufacturing to control basic functions. It runs before the operating system and is stored in non-volatile memory.
Game Dev Term 2 of 4
Game Engine
Say it out loud or write it down.
Game Dev Term 2 of 4
A game engine is a software framework designed for the creation and development of video games. It handles the core systems every game needs — rendering, physics, audio, input — so developers don't have to build those from scratch. Unity and Unreal Engine are two of the most widely used examples.

Tech Term 6 of 9
Hardware
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 6 of 9
Hardware refers to the physical components of a computer or device — the parts you can actually touch. Processor, screen, keyboard, circuit board, hard drive — all hardware. Software runs on hardware. Firmware tells hardware how to start up.

Game Dev Term 3 of 4
Frame rate is how many individual images (frames) your screen displays per second, measured in FPS. Higher frame rate = smoother gameplay. Most games target 30 or 60 FPS. Your GPU — a piece of hardware — is primarily responsible for maintaining it.

Tech Term 7 of 9
BIOS
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 7 of 9
BIOS stands for Basic Input/Output System. It's firmware built into personal computers that runs the moment you press the power button — before the operating system loads. BIOS initializes your hardware, checks that everything is working, then hands control over to the OS.

Tech Term 8 of 9
UEFI
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 8 of 9
UEFI (Unified Extensible Firmware Interface) is the modern replacement for BIOS. Like BIOS, it's firmware that runs at startup to initialize hardware before the OS loads — but with a more advanced interface, support for larger drives, faster boot times, and better security. Most computers made after 2012 use UEFI instead of BIOS.

Game Dev Term 4 of 4
Rendering
Say it out loud or write it down.
Game Dev Term 4 of 4
Rendering is the process of generating an image from a model by means of computer programs. In games, the engine takes all the data in a scene — geometry, lighting, textures, object positions — and converts it into the image on your screen. Your GPU does most of this work. Higher rendering quality = more realistic visuals, higher hardware demand.

Tech Term 9 of 9
OS
Say it out loud or write it down.
Tech Term 9 of 9
An OS (Operating System) is the software that manages all of a computer's hardware and software resources. It's the layer between you and the hardware — letting you run applications, manage files, and interact with the device. Examples: Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, Android. Firmware loads the OS when a device starts up.
